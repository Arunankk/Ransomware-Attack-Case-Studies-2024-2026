Synnovis Ransomware Attack (2024)

Overview:

In June 2024, Synnovis, a pathology service provider for several National Health Service (NHS) hospitals in London, suffered a major ransomware attack. The incident significantly disrupted pathology and laboratory services, causing thousands of medical appointments and surgeries to be postponed. The attack highlighted the cybersecurity risks faced by healthcare organizations and their third-party service providers.

Target Organization:

- Organization: Synnovis
- Industry: Healthcare / Pathology Services
- Country: United Kingdom

Ransomware Group:

The attack was claimed by the Qilin ransomware group, a Ransomware-as-a-Service (RaaS) operation known for targeting healthcare, government, and enterprise organizations.

Attack Method:

The attackers gained unauthorized access to Synnovis' network and deployed ransomware that encrypted critical systems. This disrupted laboratory operations and prevented healthcare providers from accessing essential pathology services. The attackers also exfiltrated sensitive data before encrypting systems, following a double-extortion strategy.

Impact:

- Laboratory services across multiple NHS hospitals were severely disrupted.
- More than 10,000 outpatient appointments and procedures were postponed.
- Blood testing and pathology services experienced significant delays.
- Sensitive patient and employee data was stolen.
- Recovery required extensive incident response and system restoration efforts.

Cybersecurity Lessons Learned:

1. Secure Third-Party Service Providers

Organizations should regularly assess the cybersecurity posture of vendors and service providers because attacks on suppliers can affect multiple organizations.

2. Implement Network Segmentation

Proper network segmentation can reduce the spread of ransomware and limit its impact on critical systems.

3. Prepare for Business Continuity

Healthcare organizations should maintain tested business continuity and disaster recovery plans to continue essential services during cyber incidents.

---

Preventive Measures:

- Enforce Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
- Regularly patch operating systems and applications.
- Continuously monitor systems using SIEM and EDR solutions.
- Perform regular offline backups and recovery testing.
- Conduct third-party security risk assessments.
- Implement network segmentation and least-privilege access.
- Provide cybersecurity awareness training for employees.

My Conclusion:

The Synnovis ransomware attack demonstrates how a cyberattack against a third-party healthcare provider can disrupt critical medical services and affect thousands of patients. It emphasizes the importance of supply chain security, continuous monitoring, network segmentation, and effective incident response planning. Organizations must strengthen both their internal security controls and vendor risk management practices to reduce the impact of ransomware attacks.
